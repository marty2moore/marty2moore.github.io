Tacoma Topper Roof Rack

Constraints and Goals

  • Durable, load-bearing, low profile
  • Professional look
  • 4x cross members, each mounted to rails on the topper
  • Custom design bent 5052 aluminum brackets
  • Extruded aluminum cross members for easy mounting of any attachment

Post Mortem

  • Durability achieved
  • $430 spent on project. Comparible retail product >$800.
  • Due to rail on topper being curved, cross members are not flat. Although this may be an issue later on, spacers can be added to achieve flatness. 8x robust points of attachment chosen over 4x attachment points and flat cross members.

Truck Bed Box

Constraints and Goals

  • To protect and house permanent gear in bed of truck
  • Semi-professional look
  • Made from 1/2" and 3/4" plywood
  • Light-weight design
  • Internal structures to hold gear snug when offroading
  • Platform near tailgate to hold water cube

Post Mortem

  • Durability achieved
  • Holds gear very well
  • Black truck bed liner worked very well to keep box inconspicuous. Tinted windows on topper make box hard to see from outside.
  • Annoying to open side window and trapdoor every time gear is needed from inside the box

Modular Guitar Pedalboard

Constraints and Goals

  • Small enough to 3D print
  • Must connect pedals to each other
  • Must be modular - connect and infinite number of pedals
  • Room for wiring

Post Mortem

  • Pedals needed to be taped into place - velcro would have looked nicer
  • Held pedals nicely
  • Non-standard pedal sizes were a hassle to measure for new prints
  • Rubber pieces on bottom of pedalboard were cheap so they fell off faster than anticipated
  • Sold 20!! online
